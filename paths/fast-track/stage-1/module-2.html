Your Keys, Your Bitcoin

A Bitcoin wallet is your gateway to the Bitcoin network. But here's the key difference from traditional banking: you are in complete control.

üîë The Golden Rule:

"Not your keys, not your Bitcoin" means that whoever controls the private keys controls the Bitcoin. With a proper Bitcoin wallet, YOU control your keys.

Unlike a traditional wallet that holds physical money, a Bitcoin wallet doesn't actually store your Bitcoin. Instead, it stores the cryptographic keys that prove ownership of your Bitcoin on the blockchain.

Types of Bitcoin Wallets

There are several types of wallets, each with different security and convenience trade-offs:

Security vs Convenience

Choose your wallet based on how much Bitcoin you're storing:

$10,000+ ‚Üí Hardware Wallet
$1,000-$10,000 ‚Üí Desktop Wallet
$100-$1,000 ‚Üí Mobile Wallet
Trading Only ‚Üí Exchange Wallet

üí° Pro Tip: Many Bitcoiners use multiple wallets - a hardware wallet for savings ("cold storage") and a mobile wallet for spending ("hot wallet").

üå± Your Seed Phrase: The Master Key

When you create a wallet, it generates a seed phrase (also called a recovery phrase or mnemonic phrase).

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Information:

Your seed phrase is typically 12 or 24 words that can recover your entire wallet. This is THE most important thing to protect.

Seed Phrase Security Rules

  • DO: Write it down on paper or metal
  • DO: Store in a safe place (fireproof safe, safety deposit box)
  • DO: Keep multiple backups in different locations
  • NEVER: Take a photo of it
  • NEVER: Store it digitally (computer, phone, cloud)
  • NEVER: Share it with anyone (not even "support")

‚ö° Remember: If someone gets your seed phrase, they can steal all your Bitcoin. If you lose your seed phrase, your Bitcoin is gone forever. There's no "forgot password" button.

üì¨ Bitcoin Addresses

A Bitcoin address is like an email address - it's where people send you Bitcoin. Your wallet can generate unlimited addresses from your seed phrase.

Address Types

Modern wallets use different address formats:

  • Legacy (P2PKH): Starts with "1" - Oldest format, highest fees
  • SegWit (P2SH): Starts with "3" - Lower fees
  • Native SegWit (Bech32): Starts with "bc1" - Lowest fees, most efficient

‚úÖ Best Practice: Always use Native SegWit (bc1) addresses for the lowest transaction fees.
