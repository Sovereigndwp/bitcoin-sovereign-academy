Graduate to Multisig

Time Required: 10 minutes

üéØ When You Hit $10,000

Single-signature wallets are great for getting started.
But when your Bitcoin is worth serious money, it's time to upgrade your security.
Multisig = Multiple keys required to spend.

What Is Multisig?

Multisig (multi-signature) means multiple keys must approve a transaction before Bitcoin can be spent.

Simple Analogy

  • Single-sig: 1 key = 1 door lock. Lose the key = lose your Bitcoin.
  • 2-of-3 multisig: 3 keys exist, any 2 can unlock. Lose 1 key? Still secure.
  • 3-of-5 multisig: 5 keys exist, any 3 can unlock. Maximum redundancy.

Why Multisig Matters

  • Protection from loss: Lose 1 key? Still have backup keys.
  • Protection from theft: Thief needs 2+ keys, not just 1.
  • Protection from coercion: No single point of failure under duress.
  • Inheritance planning: Family members can hold keys collaboratively.

üéì Graduation Timeline

Suggested Upgrade Path

  • $100 - $1,000: Single-sig mobile wallet (Blue Wallet, Muun) ‚úÖ You're here
  • $1,000 - $10,000: Single-sig hardware wallet (Coldcard, Trezor)
  • $10,000+: 2-of-3 multisig (Casa, Unchained, or Sparrow)
  • $100,000+: 3-of-5 multisig with geographically distributed keys

Important Notes

  • Don't rush into multisig before you're ready
  • Practice with testnet Bitcoin first
  • Understand the recovery process before committing funds
  • More keys = more complexity (but also more security)
