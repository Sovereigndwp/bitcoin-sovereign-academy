Bitcoin in Simple Terms

Bitcoin is digital money that works without banks or governments controlling it. It's like having cash that exists only on computers, but you can send it to anyone in the world instantly.

üí° Think of it this way:

Imagine if you could hand someone a $20 bill through the internet - Bitcoin lets you do exactly that, but with digital money that can't be counterfeited or controlled by any single authority.

What Makes Bitcoin Unique?

1. Fixed Supply (Scarcity)

Only 21 million bitcoins will ever exist. This makes Bitcoin deflationary - unlike government currencies that can be printed infinitely, Bitcoin's supply is mathematically guaranteed.

2. Divisibility

Bitcoin can be divided into 100 million smaller units called satoshis (sats). You don't need to buy a whole bitcoin - you can own 0.001 BTC or even less!

Quick Math:

1 Bitcoin = 100,000,000 satoshis

So 1 satoshi = 0.00000001 BTC

3. No Central Authority

Bitcoin isn't controlled by any government, company, or person. It's maintained by thousands of independent computers (nodes) around the world running the Bitcoin software.

4. Transparent & Verifiable

Every Bitcoin transaction is recorded on the public blockchain. Anyone can verify the total supply, transaction history, and network activity in real-time.
