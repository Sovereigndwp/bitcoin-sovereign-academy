When Money Goes Wrong

In Module 1, you learned what makes good money. Now let's explore what happens when money fails to meet those properties‚Äîand why it matters to you personally. Traditional fiat currencies face three major problems: inflation, centralization, and control.

These aren't theoretical issues. They affect your savings, your freedom, and your future. Let's see how.

Problem #1: Inflation (The Silent Tax)

Remember how scarcity is essential for good money? Fiat currencies fail this test completely. Governments and central banks can print unlimited amounts of money, which dilutes the value of every dollar, euro, or peso you hold.

This isn't a bug‚Äîit's a feature. Since 1971, when the US dollar was decoupled from gold, global money supply has exploded. The result? Your purchasing power steadily declines every single year.

Problem #2: Centralization (Single Points of Failure)

Traditional money is controlled by central banks and governments. While this may seem normal, it creates serious risks when those institutions make bad decisions, become corrupt, or fail entirely.

üè¶

Central Bank Mismanagement

Central banks control interest rates, money supply, and banking regulations. When they get it wrong (see: 2008 financial crisis), millions lose their savings, homes, and livelihoods. You have zero say in their decisions.

Recent Examples:

  • US Federal Reserve kept rates too low (2020-2021), causing 9% inflation
  • European Central Bank negative rates destroyed savings accounts
  • Bank of England ¬£65 billion emergency intervention (2022)
üö®

Bank Failures & Bailouts

Your bank doesn't actually hold your money‚Äîit loans it out. During crises, banks can fail, freeze withdrawals, or be "bailed out" with taxpayer money (often your money) while executives keep bonuses.

Recent Examples:

  • Silicon Valley Bank collapse (2023) - $209 billion in deposits at risk
  • Credit Suisse forced merger after losing $5.5 billion
  • Greece bank holiday (2015) - ATMs limited to ‚Ç¨60/day for weeks

Problem #3: Control & Censorship

Perhaps most concerning: governments and banks can freeze, seize, or block your money at any time, for any reason‚Äîor no reason at all. This isn't hypothetical; it happens regularly worldwide.

üö´

Financial Censorship

Banks and payment processors can deny service, close accounts, or block transactions based on politics, geography, or arbitrary rules. No trial. No appeal. Your money, their rules.

Real Cases:

  • Canadian truckers' bank accounts frozen without court order (2022)
  • WikiLeaks cut off from Visa, Mastercard, PayPal (2010)
  • Nigeria froze bank accounts of peaceful protesters (2021)
  • PayPal routinely bans users for legal activities they disagree with
  • Russian citizens' accounts frozen globally after invasion (2022)
