Problem #2: Centralization ‚Äî When a Few Control Many

Behind every fiat currency is a small group of people with enormous power. They decide how much money exists, who gets access to it, and how the rules change.

When they pull the wrong lever, entire economies shake.

Central Bank Mismanagement

Central banks decide how easy or hard it is to borrow money. They set interest rates, control money supply, and influence the rules of banking. When they get it wrong, the ripple effects reach everyone.

Too cheap money (low rates): Easy borrowing ‚Üí Prices surge

Too expensive money (high rates): Tight credit ‚Üí Recession risk

üá∫üá∏ What About the United States?

While this may not be "wheelbarrow" hyperinflation like Venezuela or Zimbabwe, it represents a slow-motion monetary crisis. When a government owes more than its entire economy produces (130% debt-to-GDP), and when interest payments alone exceed the defense budget, the path forward becomes increasingly unstable.
