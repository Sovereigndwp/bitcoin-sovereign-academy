The Law of Conservation of Value

You can't get something for nothing

The Physics of Value

In physics, the First Law of Thermodynamics states that energy cannot be created or destroyed ‚Äî only transformed. This isn't just a scientific curiosity; it's a fundamental constraint on reality itself.

Every economy, whether ancient or modern, operates under the same constraint. Value ‚Äî real productive capacity ‚Äî requires work. It requires energy input. It cannot be conjured from nothing.

‚ö° Core Principle

Money is a claim on human energy and productive output. When you create new money without new production, you're diluting existing claims. The energy hasn't increased ‚Äî you've just created more tickets to the same show.

The Inflation Illusion

Imagine a farmer who grows 100 bushels of wheat using solar energy, soil, and labor. That wheat represents stored energy ‚Äî captured sunlight transformed into calories.

Now imagine a government that prints 100 new currency units. Have they created new wheat? New energy? New value?

No. They've created claims on wheat that doesn't exist. When those claims enter the economy, they compete with existing money for the same limited production. Prices rise. The purchasing power of every existing unit falls.

üåæ The Agricultural Analogy

You can print money, but you can't print wheat. You can declare new currency into existence, but you can't declare new energy, new food, or new productive capacity. Nature doesn't accept IOUs.

üí° What You're Seeing

When you add energy/production, purchasing power increases ‚Äî more goods chase the same money. When you print money without adding production, purchasing power decreases ‚Äî more money chases the same goods. This is inflation: the inevitable result of violating conservation of value.

Bitcoin Restores the Conservation Law

Bitcoin's supply is capped at 21 million coins. Not because Satoshi liked that number, but because scarcity requires a fixed supply.

More importantly, creating new bitcoins requires energy. Mining is proof-of-work: you must expend real electricity to create new units. This anchors digital money to physical reality.

Unlike fiat currency, Bitcoin cannot be printed on a whim. The protocol enforces scarcity through mathematics and thermodynamics. You cannot violate the conservation of value in Bitcoin ‚Äî it's built into the system at the deepest level.

‚Çø Bitcoin's Elegant Solution

Bitcoin reintroduces the energy requirement that fiat money abandoned. Every bitcoin mined costs electricity. Every transaction requires computational work. Digital scarcity is anchored in physical scarcity. The conservation law is restored.
