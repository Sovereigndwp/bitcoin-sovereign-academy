# Platform specs & adaptation notes

How to adapt one idea across platforms without copy-pasting identically.

## Nostr (primary)
- **Length:** ideal < 800 chars, hard max 2000.
- **Style:** native short notes. Line breaks do the formatting. 0–2 hashtags, usually 0.
- **Links:** 0–1, never in the first line.
- **Zaps:** value-for-value only; address in body when relevant: `sovereigndwp@getalby.com`.
- **Publish:** manually via your signer/extension (Amber, Alby, nos2x). No nsec in repo.

## LinkedIn
- **Length:** ideal 900–1500 chars (hard 3000). Room to make the full argument.
- **Style:** professional, structured, complete paragraphs. Lead with the distinction,
  then explain, then the practical takeaway. Advisor/family-office friendly.
- **Links:** one, at the end. The first ~140 chars are the hook (preview text).
- **No** zap CTA on LinkedIn. Use a soft site/demo link instead.

## Substack Notes
- **Length:** ideal 400–1000 chars. Punchy, a notch longer than Nostr.
- **Style:** one idea, conversational, ends with a light link to the site or none.
- **Links:** one max.

## X / Twitter
- **Length:** ≤ 280 chars for a single post. Longer only as a `thread` (numbered notes).
- **Style:** the sharpest version of the idea. Cut every spare word.
- **Threads:** each note must stand alone; assume the rest doesn't load.

## Cross-posting rules
- Don't post the identical text on all platforms the same day. Stagger and adapt.
- Nostr leads; LinkedIn/Substack/X reuse the strongest 2–3 ideas per week, rewritten
  to each platform's length and register.
- Keep the same *idea*, change the *shape*. The validator flags identical closing lines.
