Week 3: Saving Strategies

Goal Setting and Delayed Gratification

🎯 Why We Save Money

Saving isn't about depriving yourself - it's about giving yourself options. When you have money saved, you can make choices based on what you want, not just what you can afford right now.

  • Short-term goals: New phone, concert tickets, vacation
  • Medium-term goals: Car, college textbooks, apartment deposit
  • Long-term goals: College tuition, house down payment, starting a business

🚧 Interactive Tools Coming Soon

We're building the Savings Goal Calculator and Marshmallow Challenge simulation. These tools will help you set realistic savings targets and practice delayed gratification.

In the meantime, try this simple exercise: Before buying something you want, wait 24 hours. You'll be surprised how often you realize you didn't really need it!

💡 Quick Saving Tips for Teens

Pay Yourself First

When you get money (job, allowance, birthday), save 10-20% before spending anything else.

Use the 24-Hour Rule

Want something? Wait a day. If you still want it and it fits your budget, go for it.

Save Automatically

Set up automatic transfers to savings so you don't have to think about it.
