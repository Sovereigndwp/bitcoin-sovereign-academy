The Challenge Cities Face

💸 Rising Social Service Costs

Cities spend billions on emergency financial assistance, debt counseling, and poverty-related services. These costs increase 8-12% annually as financial literacy rates decline.

📉 Declining Civic Engagement

Financially stressed residents participate less in community initiatives, vote less frequently, and contribute less to local economic growth.

🏘️ Economic Stagnation

Poor financial literacy leads to lower homeownership, reduced entrepreneurship, and decreased local business formation, limiting tax base growth.

⚖️ Increased Legal Costs

Cities face higher costs from bankruptcy proceedings, eviction processes, and collection actions when residents can't manage finances effectively.
