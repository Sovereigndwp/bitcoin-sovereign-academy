Module 6: Exchange and ETF Custody

Goal: Understand institutional custody options

Evaluate custodial exchanges, Bitcoin ETFs, and the counterparty risks associated with delegating custody to third parties.

Custodial Exchanges

How Exchange Custody Works

When a client buys Bitcoin on an exchange, the exchange holds the private keys. The client has an IOU — a claim on the Bitcoin — rather than direct ownership.

  • Advantages: Easy onboarding, familiar interface, trading features
  • Risks: Exchange hacks, insolvency, frozen accounts, regulatory seizure

Bitcoin ETFs

ETF Custody Model

Bitcoin ETFs hold Bitcoin through institutional custodians on behalf of shareholders. Clients gain price exposure through familiar brokerage accounts.

  • Advantages: Traditional brokerage, tax-advantaged accounts (IRA), SIPC coverage on shares
  • Limitations: Management fees, no direct Bitcoin ownership, no self-custody option

Case Study: The FTX Collapse

In November 2022, FTX — one of the world's largest cryptocurrency exchanges — collapsed within days, leaving billions in customer funds unrecoverable.

  • What went wrong: Customer funds were commingled and lent to affiliated entities
  • Impact: Billions in customer deposits lost; years-long bankruptcy process
  • Lesson: Exchange custody introduces counterparty risk that cannot be eliminated by due diligence alone

Discussion: How should advisors communicate counterparty risk without creating unnecessary fear?

Advisor Exercise: Custody Risk Scenario

Time: 30 minutes

Scenario: A client holds $500,000 in Bitcoin on a major exchange. Evaluate the following:

  1. What are the specific risks of this arrangement?
  2. What benefits does the client gain from exchange custody?
  3. What alternatives would you recommend and why?
  4. How would you present these alternatives without alarming the client?

Client Tool: Custody Decision Framework

A simple framework for evaluating whether exchange or ETF custody is appropriate:

  • Allocation < $50K: ETF or exchange may be acceptable for simplicity
  • Allocation $50K–$500K: Consider moving to self-custody or collaborative custody
  • Allocation > $500K: Self-custody or multisig strongly recommended

Key Takeaways

  • Exchange custody is convenient but introduces significant counterparty risk
  • Bitcoin ETFs provide familiar access but no direct ownership
  • Historical exchange failures demonstrate real risks of third-party custody
  • Custody recommendations should scale with allocation size
