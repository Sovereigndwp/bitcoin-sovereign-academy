Custody Lanes Worksheet

A one-page mapping exercise to run with the client across the desk. "Who owns it" and "who can move it" get separate answers for every holding.

How to use this worksheet

For each holding the discovery questionnaire surfaced, place it in a lane and answer the two questions that matter: who controls access, and who could help recover it. Then note the follow-up. The full comparison lives in the Custody Lanes at a Glance table.

The four lanes, one line each

  • Bitcoin ETF — exposure on brokerage rails; no direct coin access; counterparty and fund-structure risk.
  • Exchange account — platform holds the keys; convenience with counterparty risk and a platform-dependent estate process.
  • Self custody — client alone holds keys; maximum control, and no recovery if keys and backups are lost.
  • Collaborative custody — client remains the legal owner and initiates transactions; signing authority is distributed across independent parties, designed to reduce single points of failure.
