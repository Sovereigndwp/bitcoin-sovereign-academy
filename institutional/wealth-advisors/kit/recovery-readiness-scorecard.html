Recovery-Readiness Scorecard

A defensible 1-to-5 score across nine recovery dimensions. One page for the client file; the bands tell you what to do next, not what to promise.

Nine dimensions, scored 1 to 5

Score from the client's answers, not from optimism. The anchors describe a 1 and a 5; place the client honestly between them.

DimensionAnchorsScore
Existence map1 = Nobody but the client knows what exists or where
5 = A current written inventory exists and a second person knows where it lives
Access continuity1 = Only the client can act; no plan for incapacity
5 = A defined path exists for incapacity that exposes no key material
Backup integrity1 = Backups unverified or location unknown
5 = Backups exist, are separated from devices, and were tested within the year
Key exposure1 = Seed phrase has been seen, photographed, or stored by others
5 = No one but the client has ever seen key material
Estate documents1 = Documents silent on digital assets, or worse, contain keys
5 = Documents grant authority and reference a process; zero key material
Fiduciary readiness1 = Executor or trustee unaware Bitcoin exists
5 = Fiduciary knows the role, the contacts, and the first step
Family preparedness1 = Heirs either know nothing or hold premature access
5 = Heirs understand roles and sequence without holding live access
Records and basis1 = No acquisition records; platforms forgotten
5 = Cost basis, dates, and statements organized for the tax professional
Professional bench1 = No specialist, CPA, or counsel identified
5 = Named custody specialist, CPA, and counsel, and they know of each other

Total: – / 45  

Reading the bands

  • 9–18: fragile. One accident from loss. The kindest next step is a specialist conversation soon.
  • 19–32: partial. The usual profile. Pick the two lowest dimensions and assign owners.
  • 33–45: prepared. Maintain with an annual checkpoint; verify rather than assume.

The score is a conversation tool, not a certification, rating, or guarantee of any outcome.
