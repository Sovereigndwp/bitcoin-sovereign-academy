Engagement-Letter and Boundary Language

Four pieces of sample language that draw the educational boundary in writing. Every one of them goes through your counsel before use.

How to use this page

These are educational samples, not legal drafting. Bring them to your own counsel and compliance team and have them adapted to your firm, jurisdiction, and registration status before any client sees them.

Sample clause: scope of Bitcoin-related work

"Advisor may provide general educational information regarding Bitcoin, digital-asset custody models, and continuity planning considerations. Advisor does not provide custody, key-management, technical implementation, tax, or legal services with respect to digital assets, and nothing in this engagement shall be construed as a recommendation to purchase, sell, or hold any digital asset."

Sample clause: key material

"Client agrees that Advisor will not request, accept, view, or store private keys, seed phrases, wallet backups, or equivalent credentials under any circumstances. Client remains solely responsible for the safekeeping of such materials."

Sample clause: referrals and coordination

"Where Client requires custody design, recovery, inheritance implementation, or technical support for digital assets, Advisor may, with Client's consent, coordinate introductions to qualified third-party specialists. Such specialists are engaged directly by Client, and Advisor assumes no responsibility for their services."

Sample file note (after any Bitcoin conversation)

"Discussed [topic] in general educational terms. No recommendation made regarding purchase, sale, custody design, or movement of digital assets. Declined to [receive/store/view] [item], consistent with firm policy. Referred [matter] to [professional]."
