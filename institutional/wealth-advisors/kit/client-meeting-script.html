Bitcoin-Aware Client Meeting Scripts

Three meetings, each with a goal, a clock, and an exit criterion. The quoted lines are starting points to adapt, not lines to read.

Meeting 1 — Discovery (30 minutes)

Goal: a complete, honest inventory. Exit when section A and B of the questionnaire are answered.

  • Open (2 min):
"Clients have started bringing Bitcoin into planning conversations, so I've added a few standing questions. Most people have never been asked these, and a few answers usually surprise both of us."
  • Work (25 min): run the discovery questionnaire, sections A and B. Write answers down in front of the client.
  • Close (3 min): name one gap, assign no blame, book the custody conversation.

Meeting 2 — The custody conversation (45 minutes)

Goal: client sees their own holdings in lanes and owns the follow-ups. Exit when the worksheet has an owner per row.

  • Open (5 min): recap the inventory, no judgment.
  • Work (30 min): the custody lanes worksheet, one holding at a time. Use "who controls access" and "who could help recover" on every row.
  • Boundary (5 min):
"My job is to make sure every holding has a documented plan and the right professional attached. Designing the custody itself is specialist work, and I'll connect you when we get there."
  • Close (5 min): score the scorecard together; the two lowest dimensions become the agenda.

Meeting 3 — Annual checkpoint (20 minutes)

Goal: verify, not assume. Exit with a re-scored card and any changes routed.

  • Anything new, moved, sold, gifted, or inherited this year?
  • Re-score the card; compare with last year in the file.
  • Did anyone new see backups or key material? If yes, route to the red-flag protocol.
  • Documents updated? Fiduciary still aware and willing?
  • Confirm the specialist, CPA, and counsel contacts are current.
