**Status: DRAFT — awaiting Dalia's approval. D2 CTA work is blocked on this document.**

## 1. The problem this offer solves

Clients are already asking wealth advisors, tax advisors, and lawyers about Bitcoin. Most professionals face a bad choice: bluff (and risk bad advice), dismiss (and lose the client's trust), or dodge (and look out of date). None of them needs to become a custody expert — they need to know what to ask, what to flag, what not to advise on, and where real implementation work goes.

## 2. The promise

> After this program, you can handle the Bitcoin conversation with any client without pretending to be a custody expert — you'll know the questions to ask, the risks to spot, the limits of your role, and exactly when and how to refer.

## 3. Audience

Wealth advisors and RIAs · tax advisors and CPAs · estate/trust lawyers · family-office staff. (English first; an ES variant can follow the Colombia institutional track later.)

## 4. What it teaches (scope locked by Dalia, 2026-06-10)

1. Bitcoin basics — what it is, first principles, why it is different from "crypto."
2. Custody models at a high level: exchange, single-sig, multisig, collaborative custody.
3. Common client risks and misconceptions (the re-audit's misunderstanding patterns feed this).
4. The discovery questions to ask when a client mentions Bitcoin.
5. What NOT to advise on without specialist help — and why that protects the advisor.
6. Referral craft: when to route a client to The Bitcoin Adviser or another qualified Bitcoin custody adviser, and how to make the handoff without losing the relationship.
7. When to use BSA/TSA educational content with clients instead.
8. How to speak about Bitcoin credibly without posing as a custody expert.

## 5. Learning outcomes (measurable)

An advisor who completes the program can:
- Explain the custody spectrum (exchange → single-sig → multisig → collaborative) in client language.
- Run a 15-minute Bitcoin discovery conversation using the question framework.
- Identify the three highest-frequency client risks (single point of failure, no inheritance plan, counterparty/yield exposure) from a client's answers.
- State, unprompted, the boundary of their own role ("I don't design custody setups") without losing authority.
- Execute a clean referral handoff to a qualified Bitcoin custody adviser, with the engagement-letter language to match.
- Choose the right BSA/TSA resource to send a client for self-education.

## 7. Boundary rules (non-negotiable, applies to every tier)

**Allowed:** paid advisor education, workshops for wealth/tax/legal professionals, advisor readiness sessions, client-question frameworks, referral training, Bitcoin literacy for professional advisors.

**Not allowed through BSA/FSA:** designing a client's custody setup · personalized key-management instructions · inheritance planning for a specific client's Bitcoin · acting as recovery support for client funds · implying BSA/FSA is responsible for custody outcomes.

**Referral rule:** when an advisor's client needs actual custody, inheritance, vault setup, or recovery help, that client is routed to The Bitcoin Adviser or another qualified Bitcoin custody adviser. Advisor-facing referral-training materials may name TBA directly (formal referral context).

**Public CTA language (2026-06-11 policy):** consumer-facing CTAs route neutrally to Dalia's calendar ("book a call with Dalia"); advisor pages may use "Schedule an Education Consultation."

**Spelling rule:** advisor (-or) = the professionals this package teaches; Adviser (-er) = The Bitcoin Adviser and the custody-service role.
