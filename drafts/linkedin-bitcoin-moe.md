When someone asks me whether Bitcoin is becoming a medium of exchange, I usually answer with a question first: which Bitcoin, and on which rail?

The conversation collapses three different problems into one. *Store of value* is a property of the asset — scarcity, verifiability, credible absence of dilution. *Medium of exchange* is a property of the payment surface — speed, cost, merchant acceptance. *Unit of account* is a property of the application layer — wages, leases, debt instruments. Gold solved the first better than the second. Dollars solve the second better than the first.

My read, after the v4 red-team audit of the foundational-layer thesis: Bitcoin's monetary-anchor function is intact and getting stronger. Its medium-of-exchange function is real but layered — self-custodial Lightning, Fedimint at the community scale, Liquid + Taproot Assets for non-custodial dollar access, Stacks sBTC for BTC-denominated credit. Each has a different trust profile.

And meaningfully smaller than the loudest claims. Tron alone moved roughly $7.9 trillion in USDT in 2025. Bitcoin-anchored stablecoin volume across all four rails combined is one to two percent of that. Calling stablecoin remittance on Tron *"Bitcoin adoption"* because the recipient eventually converts to BTC is the single largest source of inflated MoE numbers in this niche.

The honest one-liner, for advisors and families: **activity moves to the fastest rail; trust moves to the hardest asset.** The medium-of-exchange question and the custody-architecture question are the same question, observed at different time scales.
