## Phrases to confirm or adjust

These read clearly but a native ear may prefer an alternative. Left as-is to avoid guessing.

1. **"regla de medir"** (measuring stick), used across Module 4, Module 6, and the glossary. Understandable, but **"vara de medir"** is the more idiomatic Spanish for a measuring stick or yardstick. If you prefer it, it is a global find-and-replace in the ES data file and the glossary entry; tell me and I will apply it consistently.

2. **"contrapartida"** (tradeoff), used in Module 6 rows and the `tradeoffLabel` button. Correct and neutral, but slightly formal. Alternatives a teen might feel more: **"lo que ganas y lo que pierdes"** (as the row framing) or **"el costo a cambio."** The single-word button label is harder to soften; "Contrapartida." is defensible.

3. **"guardián / guardianes"** (holder / custodian) in the Trust Map. Works and is vivid, but **"quién guarda"** or **"el que guarda"** is plainer. The tool currently mixes both ("guardianes" as the noun, "el que guarda" in Module 6). If you want one consistent term, say which.

4. **"frase semilla"** (seed phrase). This is the common community term in Spanish and is fine. Some materials use **"frase de recuperación."** Your call which matches BSA's other Spanish content.

5. **"deep dive"** left in English in the hub eyebrow and a couple of notes. Intentional, since BSA uses "deep dive" as a product name. Confirm you want it untranslated in the teen context, or I can use **"exploración a fondo."**

6. **"marcador"** (scoreboard) and **"árbitro"** (referee) in Module 4. Both natural and kept. No change suggested; flagging only because "scoreboard" is the load-bearing metaphor.

7. **Module 1 title "No puedes tenerlo todo."** Natural. An alternative with a touch more warmth is **"No te alcanza para todo,"** but that leans money-only, while the module is also about time and energy, so the current one is probably better.
