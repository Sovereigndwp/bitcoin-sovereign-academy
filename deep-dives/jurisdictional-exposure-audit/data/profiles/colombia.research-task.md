## Why Colombia is first-cohort
Core to the brand's LATAM-fluent positioning and the Programa Colombia work. Civil-law inheritance (likely forced-heirship dynamics), notarial practice, exchange-rate/registration history, and an active retail crypto market make it a high-value, high-nuance profile.

## What to research, by section
1. **Basic:** civil-law system, peso, foreign-exchange registration regime (historical capital-flow controls via the central bank), political/banking-reliability categories.
2. **Tax (DIAN):** tax-residence triggers (day-count), worldwide vs territorial basis, capital-gains/occasional-gains treatment of crypto disposals, wealth tax (impuesto al patrimonio) status and thresholds, any crypto-specific DIAN guidance, foreign-asset reporting (declaración de activos en el exterior).
3. **Bitcoin regulation:** legality of ownership and self-custody, exchange status, any Banco de la República / SFC statements, the regulatory-sandbox history for crypto-bank pilots.
4. **Reporting:** CRS participation (Colombia is widely understood to participate — verify against OECD list), CARF status, beneficial-ownership registry (RUB), source-of-funds expectations.
5. **Estate:** forced heirship / legítima rules, notarial succession process, foreign-will recognition, marital-property regime, treatment of heirs abroad.
6. **Banking:** account access, dollar access, foreign-exchange registration friction for crypto inflows, de-risking patterns.
7. **Asset protection / mobility / holder implications / direction of travel:** per template.
