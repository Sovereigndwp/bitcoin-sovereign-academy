## Why Switzerland is first-cohort
A civil-law, federal, non-EU comparator with strong private-banking and family-office relevance. The defining nuance is the federal/cantonal split: wealth tax and inheritance tax are largely cantonal, so "Switzerland" is not one tax answer. This makes it the best cohort test of the "do not generalize within a country" discipline.

## What to research, by section
1. **Basic:** civil-law system, Swiss franc, federal structure, political/banking-reliability categories, the cantonal dimension as a first-class fact.
2. **Tax:** residence triggers, the private-investor capital-gains exemption on movable assets vs the professional-trader reclassification (a key Swiss distinction — verify carefully), wealth tax (cantonal), the federal direct tax vs cantonal/communal layers, treatment of Bitcoin held privately, FTA (Federal Tax Administration) crypto guidance.
3. **Inheritance/gift tax:** primarily cantonal; spouse and direct-descendant exemptions vary by canton (verify; do not state a single national rule).
4. **Bitcoin regulation:** FINMA framework, the DLT Act, exchange/VASP licensing, self-custody position, the "Crypto Valley" (Zug) context.
5. **Reporting:** CRS participation, CARF commitment/timeline, AML Act and travel-rule, beneficial ownership.
6. **Estate:** forced heirship (réserve héréditaire / Pflichtteil) under the Swiss Civil Code and the 2023 succession-law reform that changed reserved portions (verify the current fractions), foreign-will recognition, marital-property regimes, foundations and trusts (Switzerland recognizes foreign trusts via the Hague Convention — verify).
7. **Banking / asset protection / mobility / holder implications / direction of travel:** private-banking access, lump-sum taxation (forfait) residency option for foreigners (cantonal — verify terms), residence/citizenship basics.

## Priority sources
