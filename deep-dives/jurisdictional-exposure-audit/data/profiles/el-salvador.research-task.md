## Why El Salvador is first-cohort
The most Bitcoin-specific policy environment in the cohort and the most prone to stale claims. Bitcoin's legal-tender status changed over 2024–2025 under IMF-related conditions; do not assume any earlier description still holds. This profile is the clearest test of the stale-claim system.

## What to research, by section
1. **Basic:** civil-law system, USD use, the current legal status of Bitcoin (legal tender vs voluntary acceptance — verify the latest law, not 2021 reporting), Chivo wallet status.
2. **Tax:** residence triggers, treatment of Bitcoin gains for residents and the specifics of any incentives or exemptions (verify current law; flag if changed under IMF agreement), foreign-asset rules.
3. **Bitcoin regulation:** the Digital Assets law and the National Bitcoin Office / CNAD framework, issuer/exchange licensing, self-custody position, any Bitcoin-bond ("Volcano") developments.
4. **Reporting:** CRS/CARF status, beneficial ownership, AML regime, source-of-funds.
5. **Estate:** forced heirship, succession process, foreign-will recognition, marital-property regime.
6. **Banking / asset protection / mobility:** dollarized banking access, the Freedom Visa / investment-residency program terms (verify current), path to citizenship.
7. **Holder implications / direction of travel:** be especially careful with "Bitcoin-friendly" — specify friendly for whom (the state's treasury posture is not the same as an individual self-custody holder's legal clarity).

## Priority sources
