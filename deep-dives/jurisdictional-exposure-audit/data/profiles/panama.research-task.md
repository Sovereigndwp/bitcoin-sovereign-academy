## Why Panama is first-cohort
Territorial-tax reputation, USD-using economy, residency-program ecosystem, and a financial-center history that draws internationally mobile holders. The interesting tension to research: a low-tax reputation against banking-access friction, source-of-funds expectations, and reporting commitments.

## What to research, by section
1. **Basic:** civil-law system, balboa pegged to / use of USD, no central bank of issuance, political/banking-reliability categories, financial-center reputation and any FATF grey-list history (verify current status).
2. **Tax:** territorial taxation principle and its limits, residence triggers, treatment of foreign-source income, any crypto-specific guidance, absence/presence of capital-gains/wealth/inheritance taxes (verify each; do not assume "no tax").
3. **Bitcoin regulation:** legality, self-custody, exchange status, the history of crypto legislation and any presidential/constitutional-court outcomes (verify; do not assert a bill is in force without confirmation).
4. **Reporting:** CRS participation, CARF status, beneficial-ownership registry, source-of-funds and AML expectations given the financial-center context.
5. **Estate:** forced-heirship rules, foreign-will recognition, foundation (fundación de interés privado) recognition and its use in succession, marital-property regime.
6. **Banking:** account-opening difficulty for non-residents, de-risking, source-of-funds burden, the gap between "legal" and "bankable."
