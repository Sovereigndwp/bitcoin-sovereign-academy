## 1. Basic jurisdiction profile

Civil-law system with dedicated statutes for foundations and trusts. Currency: balboa (PAB) pegged 1:1 to and used alongside the **US dollar**; no central bank of issue (de facto fully dollarized). De jure absence of capital controls not confirmed by a Tier-1 source this pass (Medium). Panama was **removed from the FATF grey list (Oct 2023)** and from the EU AML list; reputation is rehabilitating but residual de-risking persists. `Basic: Medium` [src-pa-mef-eu]

## 2. Tax overview

- **Territorial taxation:** only Panama-source income is taxed; foreign-source income is exempt for residents and non-residents. Resident if **>183 days** or center of economic interest in Panama. The foreign-source vs Panama-source line (e.g. remote work performed in Panama) is the practical risk zone. `Medium` [src-pa-pwc-residence]
- **Crypto tax:** no published DGI crypto guidance found. Under territoriality, foreign-source crypto gains prima facie fall outside the tax net, but this is **not confirmed by a DGI ruling**. `Unknown — open question`
- **Wealth tax:** none. **Inheritance, estate, and gift tax:** none. `Medium` [src-pa-pwc-othertaxes]

## 3. Bitcoin and crypto regulation

Legal to own and transact, but **unregulated** (a legal gray area with no dedicated statute in force). Panama's 2022 crypto law was **declared unconstitutional (2023)**; a newer 2025 bill is under review and not enacted. Self-custody is not prohibited (no VASP/custody statute exists), but no affirmative primary statement was located (Low, withheld from public rendering). `Bitcoin regulation: Medium` [src-pa-centralbanking-cryptolaw]

## 4. Reporting and surveillance exposure

Participates in CRS/AEOI (transparency rating upgraded; reports of "limited transmission"). **Not committed to CARF** by 2027. Private (non-public) beneficial-owner register under **Law 129 of 2020**, via resident agents. `Reporting: Medium` [src-pa-oecd-carf, src-pa-fabrega-ubo]

## 5. Estate and succession

Tier-2 sources state Panama has **no forced-heirship legislation** and does not recognize foreign forced-heirship rulings, **but intestate succession mandates equal shares to descendants** — so the "no forced heirship" framing should be confirmed with counsel. **Private-interest foundations (Law 25 of 1995)** hold assets outside succession claims and are the canonical Panamanian succession-planning vehicle. Foreign-will recognition mechanics and the marital-property regime were not confirmed this pass. `Estate: Medium` [src-pa-iba-estate, src-pa-pwc-othertaxes]

## 6. Banking reality

Non-resident account opening is **discretionary and harder post-2023**; enhanced due diligence and documented source of funds are decisive. Crypto-derived funds face friction even though crypto is legal — the legal-vs-bankable gap. `Banking: Medium` [src-pa-mef-eu]

## 7. Asset protection and seizure risk

Not separately sourced this pass. `Low — orientation only; requires local counsel.`

## 8. Residency, citizenship, and mobility

Residency-by-investment routes exist (e.g. Qualified Investor and Friendly Nations visas); thresholds shift over time and must be confirmed against the live immigration decree at application date. `Mobility: Medium / thresholds Low` [src-pa-pwc-residence]

## 9. Bitcoin holder implications

No inheritance/gift/estate/wealth tax plus private-interest foundations make Panama a low-friction succession platform, but intestate equal-share rules apply absent planning and foreign-will mechanics are unconfirmed. Good for self-custody holders not needing local banking; harder for crypto businesses needing licensing and banking. `Medium`

## 10. Direction of travel

- **cross-border-friendly** (Medium, for individuals/family offices/estate planning): no wealth/inheritance/gift tax + foundations. Counterevidence: intestate equal-share rules; unverified foreign-will mechanics.
- **self-custody-ambiguous** (Medium, for self-custody users): legal to own with no licensing burden, but the enabling law was struck down and crypto banking is hard.
- **reporting-heavy** (Medium): FATF/EU delisting + CRS + UBO registry. Counterevidence: not committed to CARF; CRS transmission reportedly limited.

## 11. Open questions

DGI confirmation of crypto-gain exemption under territoriality. Affirmative self-custody/VASP confirmation. Reconcile "no forced heirship" with intestate equal-share; foreign-will recognition. Marital-property regime. Current residency thresholds; dual-citizenship rule.
