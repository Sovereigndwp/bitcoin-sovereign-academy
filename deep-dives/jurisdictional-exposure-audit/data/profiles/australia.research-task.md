## Why Australia is first-cohort
A common-law, English-language, institutionally developed comparator that contrasts cleanly with the LATAM profiles across several themes: digital-asset CGT treatment, superannuation (retirement) exposure, exchange regulation, banking de-risking, reporting direction, and estate/inheritance treatment. Build it as a full profile, not a side note.

## What to research, by section
1. **Basic:** common-law system, AUD, no general capital controls, political/banking-reliability categories.
2. **Tax (ATO):** tax-residence tests, CGT treatment of Bitcoin disposals, the CGT discount for assets held over 12 months (verify current rule and rate), personal-use-asset exception and its limits, treatment of crypto-to-crypto trades, GST position on Bitcoin, any ATO data-matching program with exchanges.
3. **Superannuation:** whether and how Bitcoin can be held via a Self-Managed Super Fund (SMSF), sole-purpose test, custody/separation-of-assets rules. This is a distinctive Australian exposure worth its own depth.
4. **Bitcoin regulation:** AUSTRAC registration of digital-currency exchanges, the direction of the digital-asset-platform licensing reforms (verify current status), self-custody position.
5. **Reporting:** CRS participation, CARF commitment/timeline, AUSTRAC AML/CTF and travel-rule, beneficial ownership.
6. **Estate:** testamentary freedom, family-provision claims (a notable Australian feature — verify), probate, superannuation death-benefit nominations and their interaction with the estate, foreign-will recognition.
7. **Banking / asset protection / mobility / holder implications / direction of travel:** crypto-banking friction and de-risking history, residence/citizenship basics, accidental-residence risk.

## Priority sources
