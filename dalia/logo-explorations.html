Stroke variants — pick the canonical angle

The line you draw across

Three stroke-angle options. The canonical Dalia mark uses one of these consistently; the other two are reserved for context-specific applications (or rejected). Source image uses A.

A · rising right
Ascending. Sovereign rising. Looking forward. Matches source image.
B · level (bridge)
Horizontal. Bridge across. Equal access. Most explicit "bridge" reading for BSA.
C · descending right
Cutting down. Decisive, "look closer," sees the trap. More active/sharper feel.

My read: A (rising) is the most aspirational and matches your source. B (level) reads "bridge" most clearly but feels static. C (descending) maps tightest to your personality calibration — "sees the trap... points and says 'Look closer'" — the line cutting downward into the diamond. Strongest single pick: A as canonical, with C reserved for contexts where the "look closer" energy is wanted (e.g. provocative essays, decks).

Calibration check — your own words

How the mark maps to who Dalia is

"Calm, sharp, impossible to distract."
Diamond's geometric purity = calm. Sharp edges = sharp. The form has no distracting decoration.
"Sees the trap... points and says 'Look closer.'"
The single decisive stroke is the pointing line. It draws the eye across, asks the viewer to look at what's been cut.
"Warm, but not soft."
Dark substantive form (not soft) + warm gold cut (warm). The mark embodies the contradiction.
"Allergic to empty slogans."
Maximally minimal: one shape, one line. No words inside the symbol. No claim made.
"Cares about generational protection."
Diamond = gem = heirloom. The shape itself reads as something worth passing on.
"Education + bridges (BSA)."
The faceted diamond = multiple perspectives held in one form. The stroke = the path across. Education = the act of crossing.
Strengths
Distinctive (no other Bitcoin brand uses this). Maps cleanly to your calibration. Scales to favicon. Works on dark and light. The "stroke = Dalia, diamond = BSA" relationship is structurally elegant. Hand-drawn execution can soften the edges if needed.
Risks
Diamonds carry a "gem/jewelry" association — could feel commercial if the shading is too polished. Geometric mark may feel cooler than your existing hand-drawn Foundations style — needs hand-drawn refinement in final execution. Stroke could read as a "no" / cancel mark if angled wrong (avoid descending-left).
