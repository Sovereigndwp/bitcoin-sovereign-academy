# Youth & Families Program
## 10-week curriculum for schools, community centers, and libraries

### Program Overview
- Family budgeting and financial planning
- Teaching kids about money and saving
- Teen financial independence modules
- College financial planning and FAFSA
- First job and paycheck literacy
- Family emergency fund building

### Implementation Strategy
- Week 1 to 5 focus on awareness
- Week 6 to 10 focus on independence
- Every lesson should feel practical
- Students should leave each session saying "I can use this today"

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Make it Relevant
- Use examples learners recognize
- Parents discussing family expenses
- Avoid abstract scenarios

### 2. Make it Personal
- Every lesson connects to student's life
- Examples: streaming services, phone plans, sneakers, first car

### 3. Use Current Examples
- Coffee shop prices changing
- Ask: "What other prices have changed?"

### 4. Focus on Life Stages
- Family budgeting for parents
- Teen independence skills
- College planning preparation

### 5. Hands-On Learning
- Each session includes practical activities

### 6. Use Relatable Costs
- Streaming subscriptions
- Phone bills
- Sneakers and clothing
- First car expenses

### 7. Money Mindset
- Each lesson builds financial confidence

## Weekly Curriculum

### Week 1: Understanding Money Basics
**Goal:** Help students understand money's role in daily life

**Topics:**
- Needs vs wants
- Tradeoffs and opportunity cost

**Activity:** "The Choice Game" - Students make spending decisions with limited resources
**Examples:** Snacks, video games, saving for larger purchases
**Discussion:** How do these choices affect helping others?
**Take Home:** Simple spending tracker

---

### Week 2: How Money Flows
**Goal:** Understand income and expenses

**Topics:**
- Income sources
- Fixed vs variable expenses

**Activity:** Build a basic budget
**Students practice with:** Housing, food, transportation, school supplies, savings
**Take Home:** Printable budget worksheet for family use

---

### Week 3: Saving Strategies
**Goal:** Show why and how to save money

**Topics:**
- Short and long-term goals
- Delayed gratification benefits

**Activity:** "The Marshmallow Challenge" - Students plan for small, medium, and large purchases
**Tool:** Savings goal calculator

---

### Week 4: Emergency Fund Basics
**Goal:** Understand financial safety nets

**Topics:**
- Financial emergencies (job loss, medical bills, car repairs)

**Activity:** "The Crisis Game" - Students navigate unexpected expenses (broken phone, car trouble)
**Discussion:** How does planning change the outcome?
**Tool:** Emergency fund calculator

---

### Week 5: First Paycheck Literacy
**Goal:** Teach paycheck basics

**Topics:**
- Gross vs net pay
- Taxes and payroll deductions

**Activity:** Paycheck simulation - Students calculate take-home pay after taxes, Social Security, etc.
**Tool:** First paycheck calculator

---

### Week 6: Smart Spending Habits
**Goal:** Help students make informed spending decisions

**Topics:**
- Impulse buying
- Subscription services
- Lifestyle inflation

**Activity:** "The 24-Hour Rule" - Students practice delayed purchasing decisions
**Tool:** Monthly subscription tracker

---

### Week 7: Banking and Digital Payments
**Goal:** Understand modern banking

**Topics:**
- Checking accounts
- Debit cards and digital payments
- Online safety

**Activity:** Payment method comparison - Students evaluate different payment options
**Examples:** Online purchases, splitting bills with friends, large purchases
**Tool:** Safe banking checklist

---

### Week 8: College Financial Planning
**Goal:** Help families prepare for college costs

**Topics:**
- College costs and financial aid
- Scholarships and FAFSA process

**Activity:** College cost calculator - Students research and compare college expenses
**Discussion:** How do different choices affect financial outcomes?
**Tool:** College planning worksheet

---

### Week 9: Teen Independence Preparation
**Goal:** Prepare teens for financial independence

**Topics:**
- Independent budgeting
- Rent, utilities, insurance basics

**Activity:** "The Real World" simulation - Students manage a monthly budget
**Tool:** First apartment budget template

---

### Week 10: Family Financial Planning
**Goal:** Bring families together for financial discussions

**Topics:**
- Family savings goals
- Emergency planning
- Future planning

**Activity:** Family financial meeting - Parents and teens create shared savings goals, budget plans, emergency plans
**Tool:** Family financial planning worksheet

---

## Supporting Tools

**Create practical tools for each week:**

- College cost planner
- First apartment budget
- Family financial meeting guide

## Engagement Strategies

### Scenario-Based Learning
Use real-life scenarios throughout:
- Lost job situation
- Unexpected medical bills
- Scholarship opportunities
- High school graduation planning

### Decision Points
Ask students to make choices:
- "Would you spend $50 on sneakers or save for a car?"
- "Have $500 emergency fund or new phone?"

### Family Homework
Simple assignments that involve families:
- "Ask parents about one major household expense"
- "Discuss one family financial goal"

---

## Success Measurement

**Students should be able to:**
- Create a simple personal budget
- Explain the difference between needs and wants
- Build a basic savings plan
- Understand paycheck deductions
- **Families should:**
  - Start regular money conversations
  - Track household expenses
  - Have emergency fund discussions

---

## Optional Extended Modules
