What Can You Truly Own?

You learned about counterparty risk with debt. Now apply that same thinking to your assets — who actually controls your wealth?

📚 What You Already Know

In Module 3 (Banking) and Module 5 (Debt), you discovered that every financial relationship involves trust. Banks can freeze accounts. Lenders can change terms. Counterparty risk means someone else has power over your money.

The deeper question: Are there assets where you are the only counterparty?

Scenario: A Government Freezes Bank Accounts

Click each asset to see what happens to YOUR access

🏦 Bank Savings Account

$50,000 in a regulated savings account at a major bank.

Access: Frozen. Banks comply with government orders. Your funds are inaccessible until the order is lifted. This happened in Canada (2022), Greece (2015), Cyprus (2013), and Lebanon (2019-present).

📈 Brokerage Account (Stocks)

$50,000 in index funds at a major brokerage.

Access: Frozen. Brokerages are regulated financial institutions and must comply with freeze orders. Your shares exist as entries in their database.

🏠 Real Estate

A home worth $300,000 with no mortgage.

Access: Partially safe. You can still live in it, but you can't sell it easily. Liens can be placed. Property can be seized through legal process. Not liquid in a crisis.

🪙 Physical Gold (in your safe)

$50,000 in gold coins in a home safe.

Access: Maintained. Physical gold in your possession can't be frozen digitally. However, it's hard to transport, verify, and spend. Gold confiscation has happened historically (US, 1933).

₿ Bitcoin (self-custody)

$50,000 in Bitcoin on a hardware wallet you control.

Access: Fully maintained. No one can freeze, seize, or block your Bitcoin if you hold your own keys. You can send it anywhere in the world in minutes. The trade-off: you are solely responsible for security.

💡 Key Takeaway

Self-custody isn't just a Bitcoin concept — it's a financial literacy concept. Understanding who controls your assets is fundamental to financial sovereignty. Bitcoin simply offers the highest degree of digital self-custody ever created.
