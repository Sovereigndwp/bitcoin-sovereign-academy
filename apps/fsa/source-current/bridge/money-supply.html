Is Your Money Really Yours?

You've learned to invest wisely. But what happens when the measuring stick itself keeps shrinking?

📚 What You Already Know

In Module 7 (Investing), you learned about stocks, bonds, and index funds — how to grow your wealth over time. But all those gains are measured in dollars.

The uncomfortable question: What happens to your returns when the total supply of dollars keeps expanding? If $1 today buys less than $1 last year, are your gains real — or are you just running on a treadmill?

Two Monetary Designs

🏛️

U.S. Dollar (Fiat)

  • 📈 Supply: unlimited — set by Federal Reserve
  • 🖨️ 40% of all dollars were created 2020-2022
  • 🔒 You cannot audit the supply yourself
  • 📉 Purchasing power: −97% since 1913
  • ⚖️ Benefits borrowers over savers
₿

Bitcoin

  • 🔢 Supply: hard cap of 21,000,000 BTC
  • ⛏️ New supply halves every ~4 years
  • 🔍 Anyone can verify supply on a $50 computer
  • 📊 Deflationary by design after 2140
  • ⚖️ Same rules for everyone — no exceptions

💡 Key Takeaway

This isn't about politics or conspiracy. It's math. When the supply of a unit expands faster than the economy it measures, each existing unit buys less. Understanding monetary policy is essential financial literacy — yet it's rarely taught in school. Bitcoin was designed specifically as an alternative: money with rules instead of rulers.
