ðŸ’¡ Understanding Bitcoin Units

  • Satoshi (sat): The smallest unit of Bitcoin. Named after Satoshi Nakamoto, Bitcoin's creator.
  • 1 Bitcoin = 100,000,000 satoshis - Think of it like 1 dollar = 100 cents.
  • Bits (Î¼BTC): A convenient middle denomination. 1 bit = 100 sats = 0.000001 BTC.
  • mBTC (milli-Bitcoin): One-thousandth of a Bitcoin. Useful for everyday transactions.
  • Why so many units? As Bitcoin's value increases, smaller denominations become more practical for daily use.
  • Precision matters: Bitcoin has 8 decimal places of divisibility. Always double-check amounts!
  • Sats are the future: Many Bitcoiners prefer pricing in sats to normalize small transactions.
