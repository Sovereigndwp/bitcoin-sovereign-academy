ü§î What If... (Common Questions)
What if someone sees my public key?

No problem! Your public key is meant to be shared. It's mathematically impossible to derive your private key from your public key. That's the whole point of elliptic curve cryptography - easy one way, impossible the other way. Think of it like your house address - anyone can see it, but they can't get inside without the key.

What if I lose one word from my seed phrase?

Big problem - but there's hope! With modern computers, you can try all 2048 possible words for the missing position (takes a few hours to a day). But losing 2+ words? Much harder - 2048¬≤ = 4 million combinations. This is why you must write down ALL words carefully and store them safely!

What if I type my address wrong when receiving Bitcoin?

Addresses have checksums! If you type one character wrong, the checksum won't match and the wallet will reject it. This prevents most typos. However, always double-check addresses (or use QR codes) because if you make multiple mistakes that somehow create a valid checksum, the Bitcoin goes to that address and is lost forever.

You're more likely to win the lottery 10 times in a row.
