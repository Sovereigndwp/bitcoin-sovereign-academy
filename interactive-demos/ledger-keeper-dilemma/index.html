🔄 The Dilemma Reloaded

Every payment system is a story about trust.

For centuries, we trusted banks to keep our balances. Then we trusted governments to back those banks. Now we trust networks, algorithms, and code.

But here's what hasn't changed: Someone, somewhere, is writing in the ledger.

The question isn't if we trust someone—it's why we stop checking.

🇨🇴 Case Study: Colombia & Bre-B

Let's ground this in reality. Colombia is developing Bre-B, inspired by Brazil's successful Pix system.

Meet María: A Street Vendor in Bogotá

María sells arepas on the street. Let's see how Bre-B changes her life—for better and worse.

📦 Before Bre-B

  • 💵 Cash-only business
  • 📝 No transaction records
  • 🔓 No government visibility
  • 💰 Limited by physical cash
  • 😰 Vulnerable to theft

📱 With Bre-B (Benefits)

  • ✅ Instant digital payments
  • 📱 QR code on her cart
  • 🔒 No cash handling risk
  • 🌎 Receives remittances instantly
  • 🏦 Access to financial system

⚠️ With Bre-B (Trade-offs)

  • 👁️ Every sale is recorded
  • 📊 Tax authorities can track
  • 🚫 Account can be frozen
  • 🕸️ Financial graph is visible
  • 🔐 Money can be restricted
The Tension:
María gains access to the financial system—but loses financial privacy and autonomy.

Is this inclusion or integration into surveillance?
