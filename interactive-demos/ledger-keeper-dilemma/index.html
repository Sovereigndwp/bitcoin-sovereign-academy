🇨🇴 Case Study: Colombia & Bre-B

Let's ground this in reality. Colombia launched Bre-B (started rolling out 24/7 instant settlement on Oct 6, 2025), inspired by Brazil's successful Pix system.

Bre-B is a state-run instant rail operated by Colombia's central bank—not a CBDC, but a faster way to move existing bank money.

Meet María: A Street Vendor in Bogotá

María sells arepas on the street. Let's see how Bre-B changes her life—for better and worse.

📦 Before Bre-B

  • 💵 Cash-only business
  • 📝 No transaction records
  • 🔓 No government visibility
  • 💰 Limited by physical cash
  • 😰 Vulnerable to theft

📱 With Bre-B (Benefits)

  • ✅ Instant digital payments
  • 📱 QR code on her cart
  • 🔒 No cash handling risk
  • 🌎 Receives remittances instantly
  • 🏦 Access to financial system

⚠️ With Bre-B (Trade-offs)

  • 👁️ Every sale is recorded
  • 📊 Tax authorities can track
  • 🚫 Account can be frozen
  • 🕸️ Financial graph is visible
  • 🔐 Money can be restricted
The Tension:
María gains access to the financial system—but loses financial privacy and autonomy.

Is this inclusion or integration into surveillance?
⚠️ Policy Risk in Action:

Current withholding-tax proposal on digital payments in Colombia has drawn criticism for potentially disincentivizing Bre-B usage—a clear example of how state-run payment rails remain subject to changing political priorities.
